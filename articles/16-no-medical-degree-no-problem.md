## What You Actually Need to Know

### The 80/20 of Biometric Understanding

You don't need to understand the electrophysiology of the heart to use HRV effectively. You need to understand:

| What You Need to Know | What You Don't Need to Know |
|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Low HRV = reduced recovery | How HRV is calculated from RR intervals |
| High stress index = sympathetic activation | Spectral analysis of frequency domains |
| Poor deep sleep = inadequate physical recovery | Polysomnography interpretation |
| Elevated resting HR = increased system load | Cardiac conduction pathways |
| SpO2 drops during sleep = potential concern | Hemoglobin oxygen dissociation curves |

**Your role:** Understand patterns and implications for coaching.
**AI's role:** Handle the complex analysis and translation.

### The Coaching Lens vs. Medical Lens

| Metric | Medical View | Coaching View |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| HRV | Autonomic function assessment | Recovery readiness indicator |
| Sleep stages | Sleep disorder diagnosis | Recovery quality optimization |
| Resting HR | Cardiovascular health marker | Stress and fitness indicator |
| SpO2 | Respiratory function | Alert for potential issues |
| Stress index | Autonomic balance | Daily capacity assessment |

You're not diagnosing—you're optimizing. That's a crucial distinction.

## How AI Changes the Equation

### What AI Does for You

**1. Pattern Recognition**

AI scans weeks of data and identifies patterns you'd miss:

*"HRV drops 22% every Monday, recovers by Wednesday. Sunday evening stress may be anticipatory work anxiety."*

**2. Plain-Language Translation**

Complex metrics become simple insights:

*"Your client's autonomic balance is 68% sympathetic-dominant"*
→ *"Your client is in a chronically stressed state and isn't recovering properly"*

**3. Evidence-Based Recommendations**

AI suggests interventions based on research:

*"Deep sleep is averaging 34 minutes. Evidence suggests: eliminate alcohol, cool bedroom to 65-68°F, and try 200mg magnesium glycinate."*

**4. Alert Prioritization**

AI flags what matters and ignores noise:

*"Alert: HRV has declined 18% over 14 days with no recovery. This pattern suggests chronic stress accumulation requiring intervention."*

### What AI Doesn't Do

- Replace your coaching relationship
- Make final decisions about interventions
- Provide medical diagnoses
- Replace your judgment for complex situations
- Connect with clients emotionally

**The formula:** AI handles data complexity + You provide human expertise = Powerful coaching

## The Knowledge Minimum for Data-Driven Coaching

### Tier 1: Essential Understanding (Week 1)

**Heart Rate Variability (HRV)**
- What: Variation between heartbeats
- Higher = better recovery capacity
- Lower = more stressed state
- Always compare to individual baseline
- Trend matters more than single readings

**Sleep Quality**
- Deep sleep = physical recovery (target: 90+ minutes)
- REM sleep = mental recovery (target: 90+ minutes)
- Sleep efficiency = actual sleep / time in bed (target: 85%+)
- Quality often matters more than quantity

**Recovery Score**
- Composite readiness metric
- Above 60% = good capacity
- Below 40% = recovery needed
- Guides training and stress decisions

### Tier 2: Expanded Knowledge (Month 1)

**Stress Index**
- Sympathetic (fight-or-flight) vs. parasympathetic (rest-digest)
- Chronic sympathetic dominance = burnout risk
- Balance indicates healthy adaptation capacity

**Resting Heart Rate Trends**
- Individual baseline matters
- Rising trend = stress, illness, or overtraining
- Falling trend = improving fitness
- Sudden spikes warrant investigation

**Training Load Concepts**
- Acute (recent) vs. chronic (established) load
- Progressive overload principle
- Recovery must match load

### Tier 3: Advanced Understanding (Month 2-3)

**SpO2 Interpretation**
- 95-100% normal
- Below 94% during sleep = concern
- Multiple drops = possible sleep apnea
- Know when to refer

**Respiratory Rate**
- Normal: 12-20 breaths/minute
- Elevated at rest = stress or illness
- Nighttime elevation can precede symptom onset

**ECG Basics**
- What normal rhythm looks like
- Recognize alert for arrhythmia
- Not for interpretation—for referral decision

## Where Coaching Ends and Medicine Begins

### This Is Coaching (Your Territory)

✓ Optimizing sleep habits for better sleep metrics
✓ Stress reduction protocols based on HRV patterns
✓ Training load management using recovery data
✓ Nutrition adjustments affecting biometrics
✓ Lifestyle interventions for better recovery
✓ Habit change for health improvement
✓ Motivation and accountability

### This Is Medicine (Refer Out)

✗ Diagnosing sleep apnea (you notice signs, doctor diagnoses)
✗ Treating arrhythmias (you notice alerts, doctor treats)
✗ Prescribing medications
✗ Interpreting symptoms as disease
✗ Managing chronic conditions
✗ Addressing mental health disorders (beyond general wellness)

### The Referral Language

**DO say:**
"Your data shows some patterns that would benefit from medical evaluation."
"I've noticed something that's outside my scope—I'd recommend seeing your doctor."
"Let me share this data with your physician—they should see this."

**DON'T say:**
"I think you have sleep apnea."
"You might have an arrhythmia."
"This looks like a thyroid problem."

## The Confidence Framework

### "What does this mean?"

If you don't know what a metric means:
1. Check AI interpretation first
2. Look up in platform knowledge base
3. Focus on trend direction (improving/declining)
4. Ask: "Is this outside normal range?"

### "What should I do?"

If you're unsure about intervention:
1. Review AI suggestions
2. Consider: Is this coaching or medical territory?
3. Start with conservative interventions
4. Monitor response and adjust
5. Consult if still unsure

### "Is this serious?"

If you're worried about a reading:
1. Check: Is this a consistent pattern or one-off?
2. Check: Is the AI flagging this as concerning?
3. When in doubt, recommend physician evaluation
4. Better to over-refer than miss something important

## The Professional Boundary

### Your Value Proposition

"I help people optimize their health using objective data. I'm not a doctor, and I don't diagnose or treat medical conditions. What I do is use continuous monitoring to identify patterns, make evidence-based recommendations for lifestyle improvement, and prove results with measurable outcomes. When something looks medical, I connect you with the right specialist."

**This is a strength, not a weakness.** Clients appreciate clarity about scope.
