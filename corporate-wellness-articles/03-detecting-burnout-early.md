## Introduction

By the time employees report burnout, they're often already planning their exit.

Self-reported burnout represents the final stage of a physiological process that develops over weeks or months. The body shows signs long before the mind acknowledges them. Heart rate variability declines. Sleep quality deteriorates. Recovery becomes inadequate.

**Biometric monitoring can detect these patterns 3+ weeks before employees recognize or report burnout—when intervention still works.**

---

## The Physiology of Burnout

### What Happens in the Body

**Burnout develops through stages:**

| Stage | Physiological Changes |
|-------|----------------------|
| Alarm | Stress response activated, HRV fluctuates |
| Resistance | Chronic stress adaptation, HRV declines |
| Exhaustion | Autonomic dysfunction, HRV severely depressed |

### The Autonomic Nervous System

**The ANS governs stress response:**
- Sympathetic: "Fight or flight" (stress)
- Parasympathetic: "Rest and digest" (recovery)

**Healthy state:** Balance between sympathetic and parasympathetic
**Burnout trajectory:** Sympathetic dominance → parasympathetic withdrawal → collapse

### Heart Rate Variability as Indicator

**HRV measures ANS balance:**
- High HRV = good adaptability, healthy ANS function
- Low HRV = reduced adaptability, stressed state
- Declining HRV trend = burnout trajectory

## The Burnout Detection Window

### Timeline of Burnout Development

| Week | Biometric Indicators | Self-Awareness |
|------|---------------------|----------------|
| 1-2 | HRV begins declining | Unaware |
| 3-4 | Sleep quality drops | "Tired lately" |
| 5-6 | Recovery inadequate | "Stressed but managing" |
| 7-8 | Activity patterns change | "Really exhausted" |
| 9-10 | Multiple metrics concerning | "Burned out" |
| 11+ | Physiological collapse | Considering resignation |

### The 3-Week Advantage

**Biometric detection:**
- Catches decline at weeks 1-3
- Intervention at high-success stage
- Prevention rather than reaction

**Self-report detection:**
- Catches at weeks 9-10+
- Intervention at low-success stage
- Damage already done

## Key Biometric Indicators

### Heart Rate Variability

**Pattern:** Progressive decline over 2-4 weeks

| HRV Change | Interpretation |
|------------|---------------|
| 5-10% decline | Early warning |
| 10-15% decline | Developing concern |
| 15-20% decline | Significant burnout risk |
| >20% decline | Critical intervention needed |

### Resting Heart Rate

**Pattern:** Gradual elevation from personal baseline

| RHR Change | Interpretation |
|------------|---------------|
| 3-5 BPM elevation | Monitor |
| 5-8 BPM elevation | Concern |
| >8 BPM elevation | Significant stress |

### Sleep Quality

**Patterns indicating burnout:**
- Decreased deep sleep percentage
- Increased awakenings
- Reduced sleep efficiency
- Delayed sleep onset
- Early morning waking

### Recovery Patterns

**Key indicator:** Weekend/rest day recovery

| Pattern | Interpretation |
|---------|---------------|
| Full weekend recovery | Healthy stress cycle |
| Partial weekend recovery | Accumulating stress |
| No weekend recovery | Burnout trajectory |
| Worse on weekends | Advanced burnout |

## Detection Algorithms

### Multi-Signal Analysis

**Effective detection uses multiple signals:**

| Signal Weight | Metric |
|--------------|--------|
| 35% | HRV trend |
| 25% | Sleep quality trend |
| 20% | Resting HR trend |
| 20% | Recovery patterns |

### Pattern Recognition

**Algorithm looks for:**
- Sustained decline (not daily fluctuation)
- Multi-metric convergence
- Departure from personal baseline
- Comparison to known burnout patterns

### False Positive Management

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Illness effects | Filter for sickness patterns |
| Exercise impact | Adjust for training load |
| Life events | Context awareness |
| Individual variation | Personal baseline comparison |

## Intervention at Detection

### Tier 1: Early Warning (Weeks 1-3)

**Detection:** Mild pattern changes

**Response:**
- Automated wellness resources
- Self-directed stress management
- General awareness content
- No human outreach yet

### Tier 2: Developing Concern (Weeks 3-5)

**Detection:** Clearer trajectory forming

**Response:**
- Optional coaching check-in
- Stress management resources
- Sleep improvement guidance
- Manager awareness (optional)

### Tier 3: Significant Concern (Weeks 5-8)

**Detection:** Multi-metric decline

**Response:**
- Proactive coaching outreach
- Workload discussion recommended
- Manager notification (with consent)
- Professional resources offered

### Tier 4: Critical (Week 8+)

**Detection:** Severe patterns

**Response:**
- Immediate support outreach
- HR involvement
- Professional mental health referral
- Workload intervention required
