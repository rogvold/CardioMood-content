## Introduction

The American Institute of Stress reports that workplace stress contributes to 32% of healthcare costs. Add poor sleep, sedentary behavior, and untreated mental health conditions, and wellness-modifiable factors drive a significant majority of employer health spending.

**Wellness programs don't just make employees feel better—they directly reduce medical claims.**

## Chronic Disease: The Major Driver

### The 80/20 Rule

**80% of healthcare costs come from 20% of employees—those with chronic conditions.**

| Condition | Annual Cost Premium |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Diabetes | $8,000-12,000 |
| Heart disease | $10,000-15,000 |
| COPD | $6,000-10,000 |
| Multiple conditions | $15,000-30,000 |

### Prevention Impact

| Approach | Cost Impact |
|----------|------------|
| Prevent condition onset | 100% avoided |
| Catch early, manage well | 40-60% reduction |
| Manage existing condition | 20-30% reduction |
| Complications prevention | 50-70% reduction |
