## Introduction

You know biometric wellness programs work. Your executives need convincing data, clear ROI, and a compelling narrative.

Building a successful business case requires speaking the language of business: investment, return, risk mitigation, and competitive advantage. This guide walks through building an executive-ready proposal.

## The Executive Summary Framework

### Opening Statement

**Lead with impact:**
"This proposal requests [investment amount] for a biometric wellness program projected to return [X:1] through healthcare cost reduction, productivity improvement, and turnover prevention."

### Key Points

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|

## Addressing Executive Concerns

### "What if employees don't participate?"

**Response:**
- Engagement strategies proven to achieve X%
- Incentive structure drives participation
- Experience from similar companies: X% participation
- Phased approach allows optimization

### "How do we protect privacy?"

**Response:**
- Voluntary, opt-in participation
- Individual data controlled by employee
- Employer sees only aggregate trends
- HIPAA-compliant platform
- Clear policies, transparent communication

### "What's the risk if we don't invest?"

**Response:**
- Healthcare cost trend: +X%/year
- Turnover cost: $X/year
- Productivity loss: $X/year
- Competitive disadvantage: talent attraction

### "Why now?"

**Response:**
- Technology mature and proven
- Employee expectations increasing
- Competitors implementing
- Best talent expects wellness benefits
- Delay costs compound

## Key Takeaways

1. **Lead with business impact:** Investment and return
2. **Know your audience:** CFO, CEO, CHRO priorities differ
3. **Quantify everything:** Numbers are persuasive
4. **Address concerns proactively:** Privacy, participation, risk
5. **Include evidence:** Research, benchmarks, cases
6. **Offer pilot option:** Reduce decision risk
7. **Prepare for questions:** Anticipate and practice
8. **Close with recommendation:** Clear ask
