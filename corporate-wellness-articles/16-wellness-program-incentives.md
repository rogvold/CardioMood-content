## Introduction

The right incentives can triple participation. The wrong ones waste money and create resentment. Understanding what motivates different employees—and structuring incentives to drive sustained engagement—is essential for wellness program success.

## Incentive Structure Design

### Participation vs. Outcomes

| Approach | Example | Pros | Cons |
|----------|---------|------|------|
| Participation | Enroll = reward | Easy, inclusive | No outcome link |
| Outcome | Achieve X = reward | Outcome-tied | May exclude some |
| Progress | Improve X% = reward | Encourages all | More complex |

### Tiered Rewards

| Tier | Achievement | Reward |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Bronze | Basic participation | $50 |
| Silver | Regular engagement | $150 |
| Gold | Strong performance | $300 |
| Platinum | Excellence | $500 |

### Points-Based Systems

| Activity | Points |
|----------|--------|
| Daily wearable sync | 5 |
| Weekly goal met | 25 |
| Challenge completed | 100 |
| Health improvement | 200 |
| Points = rewards | Variable |

## Legal Considerations

### HIPAA/ADA Compliance

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Voluntary | Cannot require participation |
| Reasonable alternative | For those unable to meet standard |
| Confidentiality | Health data protected |
| Max incentive | Generally 30% of coverage cost |

### Best Practices

- Clear eligibility criteria
- Alternative pathways
- Non-discrimination
- Documentation

## Measuring Incentive ROI

### Participation Impact

| Metric | Measurement |
|--------|-------------|
| Enrollment rate | With vs. without incentive |
| Engagement depth | Higher tiers achieved |
| Retention | Continued participation |
| Outcomes | Health improvement |

### Cost-Effectiveness

| Calculation | Formula |
|-------------|---------|
| Cost per participant | Total incentives / participants |
| Value generated | Healthcare savings + productivity |
| Incentive ROI | (Value - Cost) / Cost |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Premium reduction works best:** Highest participation driver
2. **Meaningful value required:** $300+ annual for real impact
3. **Segment appropriately:** Different people, different motivators
4. **Tier structure:** Reward progression, not just participation
5. **Legal compliance:** ADA, HIPAA requirements
6. **Measure impact:** Track incentive ROI
7. **Balance cost and effectiveness:** Right-size investment
8. **Refresh periodically:** Incentive fatigue is real
