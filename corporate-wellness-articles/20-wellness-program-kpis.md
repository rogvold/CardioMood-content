## Introduction

You can't improve what you don't measure. But measuring everything creates noise, not insight. The right KPIs focus attention on what matters: participation, engagement, health outcomes, and business impact.

---

## KPI Framework

### Level 1: Participation

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| Enrollment rate | Enrolled / eligible | >60% |
| Activation rate | Active / enrolled | >80% |
| Completion rate | Complete / started | >60% |

### Level 2: Engagement

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| Daily active users | Active today / enrolled | >40% |
| Weekly engagement | Days active / week | >4 |
| Feature usage | Features used / available | >50% |
| Challenge participation | Joined / enrolled | >40% |

### Level 3: Health Outcomes

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| Activity improvement | Step increase | >10% |
| Sleep improvement | Quality increase | >15% |
| Stress reduction | Stress score decrease | >10% |
| Biometric improvement | Various measures | Defined targets |

### Level 4: Business Impact

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| Healthcare cost trend | YoY PEPM change | Below industry |
| Absenteeism | Sick days per employee | Decrease |
| Turnover | Voluntary turnover rate | Decrease |
| Productivity | Defined metrics | Improvement |

## Reporting Cadence

| Report | Audience | Frequency |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Dashboard | Program team | Real-time |
| Summary | HR leadership | Monthly |
| Executive | C-suite | Quarterly |
| Business review | Board | Annually |

---

## Data Visualization

### Effective Dashboards

| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Headline metrics | At-a-glance status |
| Trends | Direction over time |
| Comparisons | Vs. target, previous |
| Drill-down | Detail access |

### Avoiding Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Too many metrics | Focus on key 5-10 |
| Vanity metrics | Tie to outcomes |
| No context | Include benchmarks |
| Stale data | Regular updates |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Four levels:** Participation, engagement, outcomes, business
2. **Focus on few:** 5-10 KPIs maximum
3. **Right cadence:** Daily to annual reporting
4. **Trend matters:** Direction over snapshot
5. **Benchmark:** Compare to targets and industry
6. **Visualize well:** Dashboards that communicate
7. **Tie to outcomes:** Not vanity metrics
8. **Iterate:** Refine KPIs over time
