## Introduction

Sleep isn't just personal—it's organizational. A chronically sleep-deprived workforce experiences reduced cognitive function, impaired decision-making, increased errors, and elevated health risks. Yet sleep remains the most overlooked wellness dimension.

---

## The Performance Impact

### Cognitive Effects of Sleep Deprivation

| Hours of Sleep | Cognitive Impairment |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Less than 6 hours | 24% slower reaction time |
| Less than 5 hours | Similar to legal intoxication |
| Chronic deficit | Cumulative impairment |

### Workplace Consequences

| Impact Area | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| Decision quality | Impaired judgment |
| Creativity | Reduced innovation |
| Error rate | Increased mistakes |
| Safety | Accident risk elevation |
| Emotional regulation | Interpersonal conflict |

## What Biometrics Reveal

### Sleep Quality Metrics

| Metric | Insight |
|--------|---------|
| Sleep duration | Total hours |
| Sleep efficiency | Time asleep vs. in bed |
| Deep sleep | Restorative percentage |
| REM sleep | Cognitive processing |
| Overnight HRV | Recovery quality |

### Warning Patterns

| Pattern | Concern |
|---------|---------|
| Duration under 6 hours | Chronic deficit |
| Efficiency under 80% | Quality problem |
| Low overnight HRV | Poor recovery |
| Inconsistent timing | Circadian disruption |

## Organizational Factors

### What Hurts Sleep

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| After-hours emails | Activation, anxiety |
| Early meetings | Forces early wake |
| Shift work | Circadian disruption |
| Travel demands | Jet lag, schedule chaos |
| High stress | Sleep onset/maintenance issues |

### What Helps Sleep

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Flexible schedules | Match chronotype |
| Clear boundaries | Evening protection |
| Meeting-free mornings | Sleep-in flexibility |
| Travel recovery time | Jet lag adjustment |
| Stress management | Better sleep quality |
