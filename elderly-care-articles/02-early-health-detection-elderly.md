## Introduction

What if you could know your parent was getting sick before they felt unwell? Continuous biometric monitoring makes this possible—detecting physiological changes 2-3 days before symptoms appear. This window transforms reactive crisis management into proactive care.

---

## How Early Detection Works

### The Physiology of Decline

| Stage | Timing | Visibility |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Cellular changes | Days before | Invisible |
| Autonomic response | 2-3 days before | Detectable via HRV |
| Subclinical symptoms | 1-2 days before | Subtle vital signs |
| Symptoms | Day 0 | Patient notices |
| Crisis | Variable | ER visit |

### What AI Detects

| Metric | Pattern | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Heart rate variability | Declining trend | Autonomic stress |
| Resting heart rate | Elevated | Body fighting something |
| Sleep patterns | Disruption | Systemic response |
| Activity levels | Reduced | Body conserving energy |

## The Early Warning System

### Alert Levels

| Level | Pattern | Response |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Attention | Minor deviation | Monitor closely |
| Concern | Significant change | Check on patient |
| Alert | Multiple indicators | Contact doctor |
| Urgent | Severe patterns | Seek immediate care |

### What Triggers Alerts

| Trigger | Threshold |
|---------|-----------|
| HRV decline | >15% below personal baseline |
| Resting HR increase | >10 bpm above baseline |
| Sleep disruption | Quality score decline |
| Activity reduction | Significant drop from normal |
| Combined patterns | Multiple indicators align |
