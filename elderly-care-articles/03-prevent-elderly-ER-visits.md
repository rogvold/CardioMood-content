## Introduction

Every ER visit for an elderly patient represents a failure—not of the patient, but of the system. Most visits are the endpoint of gradual decline that could have been caught earlier. The cost isn't just financial; it's physical, emotional, and often a turning point toward dependence.

---

## The True Cost of ER Visits

### Financial Impact

| Cost Component | Amount |
|----------------|--------|
| Average ER visit | $3,000-10,000 |
| With admission | $15,000-50,000+ |
| 30-day readmission | Additional $15,000+ |
| Post-hospitalization care | Variable |
| Lost independence | Priceless |

### Physical Toll

| Impact | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| Hospital-acquired infection | 5-10% of hospitalizations |
| Delirium | Up to 50% of hospitalized elderly |
| Deconditioning | Rapid muscle loss |
| Falls during stay | Common in unfamiliar environment |
| Medication errors | Increased in acute settings |

### Functional Decline

| Pre-ER Status | Post-Hospitalization Risk |
|---------------|--------------------------|
| Independent | 30% decline in function |
| Mild assistance | Higher dependence |
| Moderate needs | Possible facility placement |

## Preventable Visit Categories

### Common Preventable Causes

| Cause | Prevention Opportunity |
|-------|----------------------|
| Dehydration | Activity/vital sign monitoring |
| Medication non-compliance | Tracking and reminders |
| UTI progression | Early symptom detection |
| Fall injuries | Activity pattern monitoring |
| Cardiac decompensation | HRV and vital trend tracking |

### Prevention Windows

| Condition | Detection Possible |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Infection signs | 2-3 days before symptoms |
| Cardiac strain | 3-5 days before crisis |
| Functional decline | 1-2 weeks before event |
| Fall risk increase | Days to weeks |

## The Monitoring Alternative

| Stage | Monitoring Response | Traditional Response |
