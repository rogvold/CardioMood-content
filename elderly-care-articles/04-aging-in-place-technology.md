## Introduction

Most seniors want to stay in their homes—and their families want to support that. But the fear of undetected health problems drives many premature facility placements. Remote monitoring bridges this gap: enabling independence while providing the safety net that makes it possible.

---

## The Aging in Place Challenge

### What Families Fear

| Fear | Consequence |
|------|-------------|
| Undetected decline | Crisis before intervention |
| Falls without help | Prolonged floor time |
| Medical emergency alone | Delayed care |
| Gradual cognitive change | Missed warning signs |
| Medication problems | Adverse events |

### Traditional "Solutions"

| Approach | Limitation |
|----------|------------|
| Daily calls | Only captures that moment |
| Frequent visits | Burden, still limited visibility |
| Moving closer | Disruption for everyone |
| Facility placement | Loss of independence, expensive |
| Full-time caregiver | $50,000+ per year |

## The Monitoring Bridge
