## Introduction

The average elderly hospitalization costs $15,000-50,000—and the cost isn't just financial. Hospital stays cause delirium, deconditioning, infection, and often mark the beginning of permanent decline. Preventing even one hospitalization delivers massive value.

---

## The True Cost of Hospitalization

### Financial Impact

| Component | Cost Range |
|-----------|------------|
| Average hospital stay | $15,000-30,000 |
| ICU admission | $30,000-100,000+ |
| 30-day readmission | Additional $15,000+ |
| Post-acute care | $5,000-25,000 |
| Long-term care placement | $50,000+/year |

### Physical Toll

| Impact | Statistic |
|--------|-----------|
| Hospital-acquired infection | 5-10% of admissions |
| Delirium | Up to 50% of elderly hospitalized |
| Functional decline | 30-40% experience |
| Falls during stay | Common |
| Muscle loss | 3-5% per week of bed rest |

### Beyond Numbers

| Consequence | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| Loss of independence | May not return home |
| Psychological trauma | Fear, anxiety |
| Caregiver burden | Family disruption |
| Quality of life | Often permanently reduced |

## Preventable Hospitalizations

### Common Preventable Causes

| Cause | Prevention Opportunity |
|-------|----------------------|
| Heart failure exacerbation | Early weight/vital sign changes |
| COPD exacerbation | Respiratory pattern detection |
| Infection progression | Early symptom identification |
| Fall injuries | Mobility pattern monitoring |
| Dehydration | Activity and vital sign tracking |

### Prevention Window

| Condition | Detection Possible |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Cardiac decompensation | 3-5 days before admission |
| Respiratory issues | 2-4 days before crisis |
| Infection | 2-3 days before severe symptoms |
| Functional decline | 1-2 weeks before fall/event |
