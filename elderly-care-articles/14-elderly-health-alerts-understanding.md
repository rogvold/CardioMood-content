## Introduction

An alert appears on your phone: "Attention: Heart rate variability below baseline." What does that mean? Should you call an ambulance? Wait and watch? Understanding alert levels and appropriate responses prevents both over-reaction and dangerous under-reaction.

---

## Alert Level Overview

### Standard Alert Hierarchy

| Level | Meaning | Response |
|-------|---------|----------|
| All Clear | Everything normal | None needed |
| Attention | Minor variation | Awareness only |
| Concern | Notable change | Check in |
| Alert | Significant deviation | Contact doctor |
| Urgent | Critical pattern | Immediate action |

### What Triggers Each Level

| Level | Typical Triggers |
|-------|------------------|
| Attention | 5-10% from baseline |
| Concern | 10-15% from baseline, single metric |
| Alert | >15% from baseline, multiple metrics |
| Urgent | Severe patterns, combined indicators |

## Common Alert Types

### Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

| Alert | Meaning | Response |
|-------|---------|----------|
| HRV slightly low | Minor stress or fatigue | Monitor |
| HRV declining trend | Possible developing issue | Check in, watch closely |
| HRV significantly low | Body under stress | Contact doctor |
| HRV critically low | Possible medical issue | Seek care |

### Resting Heart Rate

| Alert | Meaning | Response |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Elevated resting HR | Possible illness, stress | Monitor |
| Persistently elevated | Body fighting something | Contact doctor |
| Significantly elevated | May indicate infection | Seek evaluation |

### Sleep Patterns

| Alert | Meaning | Response |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Poor sleep quality | Disrupted rest | Ask how they feel |
| Multiple nights poor | Sleep problem developing | Discuss with doctor |
| Severe disruption | Possible medical cause | Evaluation needed |

### Activity Levels

| Alert | Meaning | Response |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Activity below normal | Less active day | Check in |
| Significant reduction | May indicate illness/weakness | Ask how they're feeling |
| Minimal activity | Something may be wrong | Contact or visit |

## Reading Combined Signals

### Multiple Alerts Together

| Pattern | Possible Meaning |
|---------|------------------|
| Low HRV + elevated HR | Illness developing |
| Poor sleep + reduced activity | Health decline |
| Multiple declining trends | Significant concern |
| All indicators stable | Isolated anomaly likely |

### Context Matters

| Situation | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
| After travel | Expect some disruption |
| During illness | Elevated alerts normal |
| After activity change | Baseline adjusting |
| Seasonal change | Some variation expected |

## Response Decision Tree

### When to Wait and Watch

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Single minor deviation | Check next day |
| Expected cause (travel, activity) | Monitor for improvement |
| Quickly returns to normal | No action needed |
| Parent reports feeling fine | Continue monitoring |

### When to Check In

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Multiple attention alerts | Phone call |
| Pattern persisting | Ask specific questions |
| Something feels off | Trust your instinct |
| Want more information | Have a conversation |

### When to Contact Doctor

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Significant deviation | Schedule appointment |
| Multi-day pattern | Share data with doctor |
| Combined indicators | Medical evaluation |
| Parent reports symptoms | Confirm with provider |

### When to Seek Immediate Care

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Urgent alert | Call or visit immediately |
| Multiple critical indicators | Emergency services if needed |
| Cannot reach parent | Check in person |
| Parent reports emergency symptoms | Call 911 |

## Building Understanding

### Learning Your Parent's Patterns

| Process | Benefit |
|---------|---------|
| First month | Establish baseline |
| Ongoing | Learn their normal variations |
| Over time | Recognize meaningful vs. noise |
| Experience | Better judgment on alerts |

### Questions to Ask

| When Alert Appears | Ask |
|-------------------|-----|
| Sleep alert | "How did you sleep? How do you feel?" |
| Activity alert | "Did you get out much today? Feeling okay?" |
| Vital sign alert | "Any symptoms? Feeling different?" |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Alert levels guide response:** Match action to severity
2. **Context matters:** Consider circumstances
3. **Combined signals:** Multiple indicators more significant
4. **Learn patterns:** Understand their normal
5. **Trust the system:** Designed for meaningful alerts
6. **Don't panic:** Most alerts are precautionary
7. **Know escalation:** When to call doctor, when to wait
8. **Ask questions:** Check in before assuming worst
