## Introduction

Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of death in the elderly—yet much of it is preventable or manageable with early detection. Heart rate variability provides a window into cardiac health that goes beyond heart rate alone, revealing autonomic function and cardiac risk.

---

## Understanding HRV in Seniors

### What HRV Measures

| Aspect | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| Time variation | Milliseconds between heartbeats |
| Autonomic balance | Sympathetic vs. parasympathetic |
| Adaptive capacity | Heart's ability to respond |
| Overall health | Systemic wellness indicator |

### Age-Related Changes

| Factor | Normal Aging Effect |
|--------|---------------------|
| Baseline HRV | Gradually decreases with age |
| Variability | Less fluctuation |
| Recovery | Slower restoration |
| Range | Individual variation remains |

### Research Connections

| Finding | Significance |

## Monitoring Elderly Cardiac Health

### Key HRV Metrics

| Metric | Cardiac Relevance |
|--------|-------------------|
| RMSSD | Parasympathetic function |
| SDNN | Overall variability |
| LF/HF ratio | Autonomic balance |
| Trend analysis | Change from baseline |

### Warning Patterns

| Pattern | Possible Meaning |
|---------|------------------|
| Declining HRV trend | Cardiac stress |
| Low morning HRV | Poor overnight recovery |
| Elevated resting HR + low HRV | Heart working harder |
| Sudden HRV drop | Possible acute event |

## Beyond HRV

### Combined Cardiac Indicators

| Metric | Adds |
|--------|------|
| Resting heart rate | Baseline cardiac demand |
| Recovery patterns | Heart's adaptive capacity |
| Sleep HR patterns | Overnight cardiac stress |
| Activity response | Heart's response to exertion |

### Early Warning Signs

| Combination | Concern |
|-------------|---------|
| HRV declining + HR rising | Cardiac decompensation |
| Poor overnight recovery | Heart not resting |
| Exercise intolerance | Reduced cardiac capacity |
| Pattern change from baseline | Individual warning |
