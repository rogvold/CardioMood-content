## Introduction

Recovery after surgery or serious illness is when seniors are most vulnerable. Complications can develop rapidly, and the window between warning signs and crisis is short. Continuous monitoring during recovery catches problems early, when they're most treatable.

---

## Recovery Vulnerabilities

### Post-Surgical Risks

| Risk | Concern |
|------|---------|
| Infection | Surgical site or systemic |
| Blood clots | DVT, pulmonary embolism |
| Cardiac events | Stress on heart |
| Delirium | Confusion, disorientation |
| Falls | Weakness, medication effects |

### Post-Illness Risks

| Risk | Concern |
|------|---------|
| Secondary infection | Weakened immunity |
| Prolonged decline | Not bouncing back |
| Deconditioning | Muscle loss, weakness |
| Mental health | Depression, anxiety |
| Relapse | Condition returning |

## Monitoring During Recovery

### Key Recovery Metrics

| Metric | Recovery Indication |
|--------|---------------------|
| Resting heart rate | Should stabilize/decrease |
| HRV | Should improve over time |
| Activity level | Gradual increase expected |
| Sleep quality | Should normalize |
| Recovery trajectory | Overall improvement trend |

### Expected Patterns

| Phase | Expected Pattern |
|-------|------------------|
| Days 1-3 | Elevated HR, low HRV, minimal activity |
| Days 4-7 | Starting to stabilize |
| Week 2-3 | Gradual improvement |
| Week 4+ | Approaching baseline |

---

## Warning Signs

### Indicators of Complication

| Sign | Possible Issue |
|------|----------------|
| Rising heart rate | Infection, stress |
| Declining HRV | Systemic problem |
| Persistent fever pattern | Infection |
| No activity improvement | Stalled recovery |
| Worsening sleep | Pain, anxiety, illness |

### When to Be Concerned

| Observation | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| No improvement by expected time | Doctor evaluation |
| Reverse in progress | Possible complication |
| Sudden change | Immediate attention |
| Combined indicators | Urgent assessment |
