## Introduction

Wearable data is most valuable when it flows into clinical care. EHR integration brings continuous monitoring data where clinicians can use it: in the electronic health record alongside other patient information. This connection transforms wearables from consumer gadgets to clinical tools.

---

## Why Integration Matters

### Current Gap

| Without Integration | Problem |
|--------------------|---------|
| Data in separate app | Clinician doesn't see it |
| Patient brings printouts | May not happen |
| Disconnected from care | Lost opportunity |
| No clinical workflow | Extra burden |

### With Integration

| Benefit | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Data in EHR | Available at point of care |
