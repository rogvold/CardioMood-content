## Introduction

Traditional health insurance is unavailable to billions of people. Complex products, high premiums, and extensive underwriting exclude those who need coverage most.

Micro-insurance offers simple, affordable protection to underserved populations. Wearable biometrics—increasingly affordable and ubiquitous—can transform micro-insurance by enabling objective health assessment without expensive medical exams.

**This guide explores biometric applications for micro-insurance in emerging markets.**

## Underwriting Simplification

### Traditional vs. Biometric

| Element | Traditional | Biometric |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Medical exam | Required | Not needed |
| Questionnaires | Extensive | Minimal |
| Doctor visit | Often required | Optional |
| Cost | $50-200 | $5-20 |
| Time | Days to weeks | Minutes |

### Biometric Underwriting Process

1. **Enroll:** Mobile app registration
2. **Pair device:** Connect wearable
3. **Collect data:** 7-14 day baseline
4. **Assess:** Algorithmic risk scoring
5. **Quote:** Instant premium determination
6. **Issue:** Same-day coverage

## Case Studies

### Case 1: Agricultural Workers

**Program:**
- Seasonal agricultural workers
- Hospital cash coverage
- Basic fitness band ($15)
- Daily premium ($0.10)
- Automatic hospitalization detection
- Mobile money payout

**Results:**
- 50,000 enrolled
- 85% wear compliance
- Claims paid in < 72 hours
- 95% satisfaction

### Case 2: Urban Gig Workers

**Program:**
- Motorcycle delivery riders
- Accident and disability coverage
- Smartphone-based monitoring
- Weekly premium ($0.50)
- Activity-based verification

**Results:**
- 25,000 enrolled
- Premium default < 5%
- Fraud rate near zero
- Accident claims verified in hours
