## Introduction

We're in the early days of biometric insurance. Wearable adoption is growing, analytics are improving, and programs are expanding. But where does this trajectory lead?

By 2030, biometric data could fundamentally reshape insurance—from how policies are priced to how health is managed to how claims are processed. This article explores plausible scenarios for the evolution of biometric insurance.

**This is not prediction. It's exploration of possibilities.**

**Traditional:** Point-in-time assessment at application
**Future:** Continuous health monitoring throughout policy life

| Element | Traditional | 2030 Vision |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Data collection | Application moment | Continuous |
| Assessment | One-time | Dynamic |
| Pricing | Fixed | Responsive |
| Intervention | None | Continuous |

### Accelerated to Instant

| Metric | Today | 2030 |
|--------|-------|------|
| Decision time | Days to weeks | Minutes to hours |
| Data required | Forms + exams | Biometric stream |
| Human review | Most cases | Edge cases only |
| Accuracy | Good | Excellent |

### Personalized Pricing

**Moving from:**
- Age/gender/occupation bands
- Medical history classes

**Moving to:**
- Individual health trajectory
- Behavioral risk scoring
- Real-time adjustment
- Engagement-modified pricing
